DIFFERENT DOG BREEDS IN 2022

Looking forward to adopting a new fur friend? The FCI (Fédération Cynologique Internationale or The World Canine Organisation) currently recognizes 360 dog breeds in 2022, so making your choice could be more challenging than you thought. Additionally, dog breeds differ in size, health requirements, and personality. To help you make your choice, we will cover some popular dog breeds and their similarities and differences from one another.

Dog Breed Physical Features Personality & Behavior

1. Golden Retriever
Golden retriever males are 23 to 24 inches tall and weigh about 65 to 75 pounds. Females are about 21 to 22 inches tall and weigh about 55 to 65 pounds. The golden retriever has a water-repellent coat that is wavy or flat and gold to cream in color.
Golden retrievers are even-tempered, intelligent, and affectionate. They are often playful, yet gentle with children, and they tend to get along well with other pets and strangers.

2. Siberian Husky
Siberian huskies are medium-sized dogs, slightly longer than tall. Their height ranges from 20 to 23 1/2 inches and weight from 35 to 60 pounds. They also have erect ears alongside eyes of brown to blue or maybe even one of each color. The neck is carried straight and the topline is level.
Siberian huskies are friendly and intelligent but somewhat independent and stubborn. They thrive on human company, but need firm, gentle training from puppyhood.

3. Aspin
Most Aspins are medium-sized dogs that stand at no more than two feet in height. Their coat is usually short and rough with varying colors. They can be brown, black, white, brindled or spotted.
Aspins are known for being incredibly affectionate and nice to anyone they encounter, especially children. They are frequently seen wandering the streets, though they are increasingly becoming house dogs now.

4. Shih Tzu
Shih tzus are often slightly longer than tall, with a sturdy body and a good-sized head. Their muzzle is quite short, with an undershot bite. The head is rounded and the eyes are quite prominent. The tail is carried gaily curled up over the back. However, they often need lots of grooming.
Shih tzu are renowned for their quirky and happy temperaments, as they are often lively and friendly. Shih tzus tend to get along well with people of all ages and with other dogs as well as other pets of different species.

5. Jack Russell terrier
Jack Russell terriers come in three different coat types: smooth, broken and rough (coarse, longer straight hair). All the coats tend to shed. Jack Russell terriers are white with black or tan markings. Both the males' and females' weight often ranges from 13-17 pounds. The breed also has a compact, muscular body.
Jack Russell terriers are happy, clever, and energetic dogs with a strong desire to work. This breed is most happy when given companionship and a job to do. Digging is also normal for a Jack Russell.

We just covered five popular breeds of dogs. When considering what dog breed you should adopt, always consider factors such as the physical features and behavior you want in your pet.
